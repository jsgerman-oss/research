\section{Future Work}
\label{sec:future}

\paragraph{Trained classifier (DistilBERT) v2.}
Replace the semantic-similarity v1 router with a trained classifier
on \(\sim\)500 hand-annotated turns. Phase-A research projects
45--50\,\% cost reduction at <1\,\% quality loss, with 50\,ms
inference overhead. Tracked at \texttt{bd blackrim-x3ju}'s parent
epic.

\paragraph{Cascade routing.}
Always try sonnet first, measure semantic entropy on the response,
escalate to opus when entropy exceeds threshold. Phase-A research
projects 40--45\,\% cost reduction at <0.5\,\% quality loss with
150\,ms overhead. Higher latency but bypasses the per-turn
classification overhead entirely on cache hits.

\paragraph{Cross-cache hit-rate optimisation.}
The prefix cache and plan cache target orthogonal cost sources, but
their hit rates are not independent: a high prefix-cache hit
correlates with structurally-similar dispatches (which would also
be plan-cache hits). Joint analysis: are we double-counting savings
in §\ref{sec:eval}?

\paragraph{Held-out instruction-following eval.}
Required for §\ref{sec:eval}'s fidelity claim to be publishable.
The eval suite should cover: routine-orchestration turns
(claim/dispatch/merge), mid-complexity turns (refactor/fix/doc),
novel-reasoning turns (architecture/debug). Build alongside
\texttt{gt eval generate} (already shipped via
\texttt{blackrim-mmlp}).

\paragraph{Comparison with auto-compact.}
Auto-compact (\texttt{blackrim-uvep}) attacks the same cost
shape from a different angle: shrink conversation history rather
than route turns. The two should compose multiplicatively. Future
work: empirical study of router \(\times\) compact composition.
