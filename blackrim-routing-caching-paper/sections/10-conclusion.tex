\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We showed that the main thread of a well-tuned multi-agent LLM coding system --- not subagent dispatch --- is the binding cost lever: a 7-day production trace of \blackrim places 99.4\,\% of session spend on the orchestrator's turns and only 0.6\,\% on dispatched workers. Two complementary primitives target this lever. A semantic-similarity per-turn router with conservative escalation achieves macro-F1 0.526 in leave-one-out cross-validation (a +6.0 point improvement over the length-heuristic baseline), delivering a 30\,\% cost improvement over that baseline at the cost of zero training overhead. An agentic plan-level semantic cache stacks on top of the existing prefix cache, targeting the structurally-similar dispatch plans that recur across bd sessions; where prefix caching already achieves 78.6\,\% savings on cache-warm calls, the plan cache addresses the remaining uncached dispatch cost. The two primitives compose multiplicatively because they act on disjoint cost factors --- tier rate, per-call multiplier, and call count --- and neither primitive degrades fidelity along the other's dimension. Both the router and the cache are reproducible from published scripts and CSVs. The companion instruction-trim paper (\cite{trimpaper}) provides a third fidelity-preserving primitive --- shrinking the static prefix --- that stacks with routing and plan caching to form a complete cost-reduction framework for static-prefix multi-agent coding systems.
