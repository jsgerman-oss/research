\begin{abstract}
\noindent
In a static-prefix multi-agent LLM coding system with a well-tuned
subagent dispatch tier, dispatched subagents account for only
\textbf{0.6\,\%} of total session cost (445 main-thread turns,
55 subagent calls, 7-day production window on claude-opus-4-7).
The orchestrator's main thread accounts for the remaining
\textbf{99.4\,\%}. Once dispatch is tuned, the orchestrator itself
is the binding cost lever---every main-thread turn pays opus rates
regardless of whether the turn requires opus-level capability.

We present two complementary primitives targeting this lever.
The first is a \emph{per-turn semantic-similarity router}: each turn
is embedded and matched against a curated exemplar set
(\(\sim\)150 labeled turns); the top-\(k\) neighbours are
majority-voted, with conservative escalation to opus below a
similarity threshold~\(\tau\). Leave-one-out cross-validation on
49 hand-labelled turns yields macro-F1 0.526 (+6.0 points over the
length-heuristic baseline of 0.466), accuracy 61.2\,\%, haiku-F1 0.786,
and an estimated 30\,\% cost improvement over the length-heuristic.

The second is an \emph{agentic plan-level semantic cache}: plans for
structurally equivalent dispatches are keyed on a stable signature
(role, task shape, normalised file scope, deliverable kind) and
cached with LRU eviction and a 30-day TTL. The cache stacks atop
the existing prefix cache. The prefix cache achieves a 10.44\(\times\)
read-to-creation ratio, 90.1\,\% hit rate, and 78.6\,\% estimated
savings on real-API spawns. Plan caching on real production data
(\(n=3\)) yields 0\,\% hit rate---a small-\(n\) result we report
transparently; a 40-dispatch synthetic fixture achieves 15\,\% hit
rate and zero false hits, confirming harness correctness.
Composed savings range from 78.6\,\% (prefix only) to 81.8\,\%
(synthetic fixture with plan cache).

The two primitives compose multiplicatively because they act on
disjoint cost factors. All scripts, CSVs, and labels are released.
\end{abstract}
