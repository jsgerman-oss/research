\section{Problem Formulation}
\label{sec:problem}

\subsection{Setting}

A multi-agent LLM coding session is a sequence of \emph{turns}
\(\turn_1, \turn_2, \ldots, \turn_N\) on the orchestrator's main
thread, interleaved with subagent dispatches. Each turn is processed
on a model tier \(\tier \in \tierset = \{\text{haiku}, \text{sonnet},
\text{opus}\}\). Per-call cost \(\cost(\turn, \tier)\) decomposes
into input-token cost (with cache discount) and output-token cost,
both indexed by tier rate-sheet. Total session cost is
\(\sum_{i=1}^{N} \cost(\turn_i, \tier_i)\).

\subsection{Empirical observation}

We measure \blackrim's cost split as 99.4\,\% main-thread,
0.6\,\% dispatch (verified 2026-05-09; see §\ref{sec:eval}). The
orchestrator dominates spend even after the well-tuned subagent
advisor (CC-TS) routes dispatched workers to their cost-optimal
tier. \emph{Main-thread tier-down is the binding lever.}

\subsection{Per-turn routing decision}

For each main-thread turn \(\turn_i\), choose tier \(\tier_i\) to
minimise expected cost subject to a quality-preservation constraint:

\begin{equation}
  \tier_i^{*} = \arg\min_{\tier \in \tierset}\; \cost(\turn_i, \tier)
  \quad \text{s.t.} \quad
  \mathbb{E}[\quality(\turn_i, \tier)] \geq \quality_0 - \epsilon
  \label{eq:routing}
\end{equation}

where \(\quality_0\) is the opus-tier baseline and \(\epsilon\) is
the per-turn quality-loss tolerance. \(\quality\) is observable
post-hoc through the same three-channel mechanism the subagent
advisor uses (\cite{moa1landscape}): structured-output correctness,
tool-use success, eval-suite scores when available.

\subsection{Plan-cache decision}

For each dispatched plan with semantic signature \(\plan\), choose
between cache-hit (\(\plan \in \planset\)) and cache-miss (compute
fresh). Cost reduction:

\begin{equation}
  \cost_{\text{cached}}(\plan) =
    \hitrate(\plan) \cdot \cost_{\text{lookup}} +
    (1 - \hitrate(\plan)) \cdot \cost_{\text{compute}}(\plan)
  \label{eq:plancache}
\end{equation}

with \(\hitrate(\plan)\) the empirical hit-rate for plans of
\(\plan\)'s shape, \(\cost_{\text{lookup}}\) the cache-fetch cost
(O(1) hash lookup; negligible token cost), and
\(\cost_{\text{compute}}(\plan)\) the standard compute cost. The
plan cache stacks on top of prefix caching --- the prefix-cache
discount applies to whichever path is chosen, since both still pay
the briefing-composition overhead.

\subsection{Joint optimisation}

The two primitives are independent in the cost equation: routing
chooses \(\tier_i\) per turn, plan caching reduces the
expected-compute term within the chosen tier. Composed savings are
multiplicative under reasonable independence assumptions.
