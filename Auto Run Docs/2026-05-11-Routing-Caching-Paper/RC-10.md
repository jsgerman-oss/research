## Tasks
- [ ] **§3 Problem Formulation.** Rewrite
  `sections/03-problem-formulation.tex`. Structure:
  1. *Setting* — define τ (turn), T (tier set), p (plan), C
     (cost), Q (quality). Per-turn we route to t ∈ T; per-dispatch
     we look up p in plan cache.
  2. *Per-turn cost model*. `C(τ, t) = α_t · |context(τ)| + β_t ·
     |output(τ)|` with α, β the tier's input/output rates.
  3. *Composed cost*. The total session cost over N turns + M
     dispatches: sum of per-turn route-decisions + sum of per-
     dispatch plan-cache hits/misses, with the prefix-cache hit
     rate ρ multiplying input-side α.
  4. *Decision-rule trade-offs*. Per-turn: tier-down iff
     `C(τ, t_low) ≤ C(τ, t_high)` AND `Q(τ, t_low) ≥ Q(τ, t_high)
      - ε`. Per-dispatch: cache-reuse iff
     `sim(p_query, p_cached) > θ_hit` AND `confidence > θ_conf`.

  2. *Existing dispatch-side routing (the analog).* The 16-rule

  1. *Approach.* Build a plan-signature index from historical
     dispatches; on new query, embed user_query, retrieve top-1

  2. *Algorithm (algorithm2e block).* Pseudocode for

  5. *Composition with prefix caching.* Plan cache fires BEFORE
     the LLM call → bypasses both cache_creation AND cache_read.
     This is why composed savings > sum of individual savings.
